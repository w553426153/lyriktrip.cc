📍 11:30-15:30 | Giant Panda Breeding Research Base
Suggested Duration: About 2 hours
Attraction Overview
Many people come to Chengdu just to see pandas. While pandas are found in many zoos, only the Chengdu Panda Base can gather over 100 pandas of different age groups at once. So coming to Chengdu without meeting these adorable creatures and having fun with them would be a pity?
The pandas there truly have a chubby-cute charm, with an innate flirtatious aura, forever wearing a look of indifferent charm, forever unbothered by others, forever entertaining themselves, eating and drinking, either lying down, eating, or lying down while eating, or walking on the path of lying down eating—forever eating!

📍 15:30-16:30 | Kuan-Zhai Alley
Suggested Duration: 1 hour
Attraction Overview
Avoid crowds and wander through the dappled tree shadows of these serene alleys to explore the city’s hidden secrets—you’ll discover another side of this city.
Consisting of Wide Alley, Narrow Alley, and Well Alley, it is the largest preserved Qing Dynasty ancient street in Chengdu. Wide Alley features the most complete old buildings in the area, where you can enjoy a bowl of tea, taste authentic Sichuan cuisine, and experience the local customs of old Chengdu. Narrow Alley contains both late Qing and early Republican architecture and early Western-style buildings, serving as a leisure lifestyle zone centered on Western food culture and artistic recreation. Well Alley is a typical miniature of Chengdu folk culture; besides the captivating brick culture wall, it also gathers Chengdu specialty snacks and folk toys.

📍 15:30-17:00 | Chengdu People's Park
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Head to Chengdu citizens’ beloved park—People's Park. Here gather the ‘flower-praising’ elders of Chengdu, each forming their own cliques, singing and dancing, a scene of peace and prosperity, reflecting the flourishing state of the motherland!
You can enjoy a cup of authentic Chengdu covered-bowl tea at the Heming Tea House, half-reclining on a bamboo chair in this century-old tea house, listening to stories from the past, enjoying Chengdu’s unique leisurely tranquility.
Inside, you’ll find genuine, real Chengdu life—leisurely, contented, at ease. The century-old Heming Tea House remains bustling with voices, ear-cleaning workers weaving through, calling out to find customers.

📍 15:30-16:30 | Ciqikou Ancient Town
Suggested Duration: 1 hour
Attraction Overview
Ciqikou Ancient Town, located near the urban area of Chongqing, was once a bustling land and water dock. Stroll along the blue stone slab roads, savor local delicacies, find a teahouse to sit and relax, and experience the folk customs of old Chongqing. Due to its proximity to the city center, the ancient town is now a popular leisure and entertainment spot for locals. The town features over a dozen alleys lined with Ming and Qing-style architecture. Visitors can enjoy local specialties such as Mao Xue Wang, Qian Zhang Pi, and Jiao Yan Hua Sheng — known as the town’s “Three Culinary Treasures.” Even more famous is “Chen Mahua.”

📍 11:00-11:15 | Liziba Monorail Through-the-Building Exterior View
Suggested Duration: About 15 minutes
Attraction Overview
In Chongqing, mountains are the city and the city is mountains. Walking through such a city without a cool mode of transport would truly be ‘hard to get around.’ At Liziba, every few minutes, a monorail glides silently along this lush green elevated track toward residential buildings.

📍 13:00-13:30 | Yangtze River Cableway
Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
The Yangtze River Cableway, located in Chongqing, China, is the city’s second cross-river cableway (the first being the Jialing River Cableway). Having operated for nearly 30 years, it is acclaimed as the “First Airborne Corridor of the Ten-Thousand-Li Yangtze” and the “Mountain City’s Airborne Public Bus.”

📍 15:00-16:30 | Chongqing Jiefangbei
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Liberation Monument is located at the intersection of Minquan Road, Minzu Road, and Zou Rong Road in Yuzhong District, Chongqing's main urban area, and is a landmark building of Chongqing. The only monument in the country commemorating the victory of the Chinese nation in the War of Resistance Against Japan, Liberation Monument's full name is 'People's Liberation Monument,' representing peace and liberation. It is a historical witness to the victory of the War of Resistance and the liberation of Chongqing.

📍 17:30-19:20 | 洪崖洞
Suggested Duration: 1小时
Attraction Overview
Hongya Cave is a great place to stroll through the old streets of the mountain city, enjoy the scenery of the two rivers, and taste local cuisine. It consists of four parts: the Jiuyan River Bar Street, Tiansheng Lane Bashu Style Street, Food Street, and City Balcony, with a total of 11 floors from bottom to top, each with different functions, including restaurants, bars, and local specialty shops. Centered on stilted buildings with traditional Bashu architectural features, it follows the contours of the mountain, offering panoramic river views from the top floor, and at night, it is brilliantly lit, truly a scenic highlight of the mountain city.

📍 19:00-19:20 | 来福士广场 游览外观
Suggested Duration: 约20分钟
Attraction Overview
The 'Chongqing Lai Fu Shi Square' is named 'Chao Tian Yang Fan,' located at Chao Tian Men where the two rivers converge, designed by world-renowned architect Moshe Safdie. The entire project symbolizes Chongqing's spirit of 'setting sail far away,' interpreting the magnificent grandeur of 'Ancient Yu's Mighty Pass,' and upon completion will become a landmark building of Chongqing. The six towers are designed based on Chongqing's shipping culture, respectively rising to 350 meters and 250 meters in height, shaped like strong sails on the river surface. The highlight of the project is the 'Crystal Corridor' connecting four towers, located at 60 floors above ground, stretching 300 meters long, with its crystal-clear glass structure bringing public spaces and urban gardens into Chongqing's skyline. Standing within the corridor, one can take in the full view of Chongqing's river and mountain scenery.

📍 20:20-21:20 | 两江游
Suggested Duration: 约1小时
Attraction Overview
"Not viewing the night scenery means not having truly visited Chongqing" — missing Chongqing's night view is a great regret. The city's unique landscape allows one, no matter where they stand, to appreciate layers upon layers of lights. Chongqing's nights are adorned with countless lights and twinkling neon, dreamy and dazzling, reflected in the rippling river waters, scattered yet orderly. If Beijing and Shanghai's nightscapes are 'grand,' then Chongqing's nightscapes are 'moving.' The 'Two Rivers Tour' ingeniously combines Chongqing's mountains and waters with its world-famous night scenery to become the city's calling card; it is a window allowing the outside world to intuitively, three-dimensionally, dynamically, comprehensively, up close, understand and recognize Chongqing, showcasing its charm. Taking a night cruise on the two rivers feels like swimming through a galaxy of stars, with lights blazing on both banks, starlight and moonlight reflected in the water, the river flowing gently, the breeze softly blowing, light tea and mild wine washing away worldly worries. Clouds flow, light shimmers, fire trees and silver flowers, myriad lights, celestial rivers and stars shining together...
