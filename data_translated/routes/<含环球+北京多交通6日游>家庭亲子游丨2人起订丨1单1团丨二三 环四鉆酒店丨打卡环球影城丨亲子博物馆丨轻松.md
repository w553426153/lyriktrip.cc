📍 15:00-15:10 | Monument to the People's Heroes
Suggested Duration: About 10 minutes
Attraction Overview
【Monument to the People's Heroes】is located at the center of Tiananmen Square in Beijing. On September 30, 1949, the First Plenary Session of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference decided to erect the Monument to the People's Heroes in the capital, Beijing, to commemorate the people's heroes who sacrificed their lives in the People's Liberation War and the People's Revolution. That afternoon at 6:00 PM, all delegates attending the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference held the foundation-laying ceremony for the monument on the square in front of Tiananmen. After extensive nationwide discussion, the monument's design was finalized. In 1961, the Monument to the People's Heroes was announced by the State Council of the People's Republic of China as one of the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units. The Monument to the People's Heroes is located north of the Mao Zedong Memorial Hall and was built by the Government of the People's Republic of China to commemorate revolutionary martyrs in modern Chinese history.

Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
【Mao Zedong Memorial Hall】is situated to the south of the Monument to the People's Heroes on Tiananmen Square. Completed in May 1977, it covers over 57,000 square meters, with a total building area of 28,000 square meters. The main structure is square-shaped, surrounded by 44 yellow Fujian granite columns, with Guangzhou Shiwan ceramic tile decorations between the columns, and the entire exterior clad in Qingdao granite. The roof features two layers of glass flying eaves, with sunflower relief carvings inlaid between the eaves. The base has two levels of **, the steps entirely constructed from Sichuan Dadu River jujube-red granite, encircled by Han white jade evergreen floral railings from Fangshan.

Attraction Overview
Explore one of the largest ancient imperial palace architectural complexes in the world—the 【Forbidden City】, including the main ticket (3-hour in-depth tour), and experience the grandeur and luxury of the imperial palace.
The Forbidden City is located in the center of Beijing, formerly known as the Purple Forbidden City. Completed in the 18th year of the Yongle reign of the Ming Dynasty (1420), it served as the imperial palace for the Ming and Qing dynasties, an unparalleled masterpiece of ancient architecture, and the largest and most complete existing wooden-structured ancient building complex in the world. All buildings in the Forbidden City consist of two parts: the 'Outer Court' and the 'Inner Court,' surrounded by city walls. It is embraced by the moat (Tongzi River) on all four sides, with corner towers at each of the four corners. Each side has one gate; the southern gate is the Meridian Gate, the main entrance to the Forbidden City.

📍 21:00-00:00 | Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution
Suggested Duration: About 3 hours
Attraction Overview
The Chinese People's Revolution Military Museum is located on the extension of Chang'an Avenue west of Tiananmen Square in Beijing, on the southern shore of Yuyuantan Lake. It was planned in 1958 as one of the Ten Great Buildings of the Capital to celebrate the 10th anniversary of National Day. As China's only large-scale comprehensive military history museum, it covers over 80,000 square meters, with a building area exceeding 60,000 square meters and an exhibition area surpassing 40,000 square meters. The main building stands 94.7 meters tall, with seven central floors and four on each side. The circular tower atop the building holds a gilded six-meter-diameter emblem and badge of the Chinese People's Liberation Army's 'August 1st' insignia, soaring high and gleaming with golden light. The 4.9-meter-tall bronze doors were cast from artillery shells sent by frontline units in Fujian during wartime.
