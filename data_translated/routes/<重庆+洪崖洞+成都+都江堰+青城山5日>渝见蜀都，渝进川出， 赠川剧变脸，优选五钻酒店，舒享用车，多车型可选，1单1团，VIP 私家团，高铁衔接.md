📅 Day 2: Zazidong a Bai Gongguan a Ciqikou Ancient Town a Liziba Monorail a Yangtze River Cableway a Jiefangbei Hongya Cave
Bai Gongguan -> Ciqikou Ancient Town

📍 13:00-13:30 | Bai Gongguan
Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Bai Gongguan is located in Gele Mountain, Shapingba District, Chongqing, a revolutionary site commemorating martyrs that evokes deep emotion. Originally the suburban villa of Sichuan warlord Bai Ju, who claimed descent from the Tang Dynasty poet Bai Juyi, he adopted the poetic alias 'Xiangshan Jushi' (the first two characters of 'Xiangshan Hermit') to name his villa 'Xiangshan Villa'. However, locals have traditionally referred to it as 'Bai Gongguan' according to local custom. In 1939, the Military Intelligence Bureau forcibly purchased it with 30 taels of gold and converted it into a secret prison. Bai Gongguan once held over 100 detainees at its peak, all deemed by the Bureau to be involved in serious cases or of high rank.

📍 14:00-19:00 | Ciqikou Ancient Town
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Ciqikou Ancient Town, located near Chongqing's urban area, was once a bustling land and water port. Walk on the blue stone streets, taste local snacks, sit in a teahouse, and experience the folk customs of old Chongqing. Due to its proximity to the city, it remains a popular leisure destination for residents today. The town features over ten lanes and alleys lined with Ming and Qing-style architecture. Local delicacies and snacks are available, including Mao Xue Wang, Qian Zhang Pi, and Jiao Yan Hua Sheng — the town's 'Three Culinary Treasures'. Even more famous is 'Chen Mahua'.
Given the abundance of snacks and delicacies in Ciqikou, you may freely choose your lunch based on personal taste today. Please note that the ancient town can be crowded with visitors.
【Recommended Food】The Mao Xue Wang opposite Qingyun Teahouse is exceptionally authentic, and their douhua (tofu pudding) is unforgettable. Local specialties include Chen Changyin Mahua and hot pot base — the freshly stir-fried version is particularly fragrant.
Liziba Monorail (Exterior View)
In Chongqing, mountains are the city and the city is mountains. Walking through such a city without a cool transportation method is truly 'hard to navigate'. At Liziba, every few minutes, a monorail glides along this lush green elevated track, slowly arriving at residential buildings.

📅 Day 3: Chongqing Chengdu a Wuhou Shrine a Jinli Ancient Street a Kuan-Zhai Alley a 《Shu Feng Ya Yun》 Sichuan Opera Face-Changing
Wuhou Shrine -> Jinli

📍 13:00-20:00 | Wuhou Shrine
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour
Attraction Overview
The temple where Liu Bei and Zhuge Liang are jointly enshrined, China's **temple for joint enshrinement of ruler and minister.
Originally referring to the hall beside Liu Bei's Hui Mausoleum dedicated to Zhuge Liang, after continuous repairs and integration during the Ming and Qing dynasties, it **evolved into an architectural complex comprising the Han Zhaolie Temple, Wuhou Shrine, Hui Mausoleum, San Yi Temple, and Jinli Pedestrian Street.
Snacks suit 90% of visitors' tastes.

📍 20:00-21:00 | Jinli
Suggested Duration: 1 hour
Attraction Overview
Jinli is an imitation ancient commercial street, one of Chengdu's most popular attractions, and also part of the Wuhou Shrine Museum. Here, you can admire clay figurine molding and sugar painting, and browse shops rich in Three Kingdoms characteristics. The street also features various Sichuan specialty snacks, tea houses, coffee
shops, bars, and inns available for seating at any time. At dusk, Jinli lights up with lamps, full of charm, ideal for photography and leisure.
Wide and Narrow Alleys
Drinking tea, bar-hopping, ear cleaning, eating hot pot — experience the leisurely, happy lifestyle of Chengdu residents.
Composed of Wide Alley, Narrow Alley, and Well Alley arranged in parallel, all featuring blue-gray brick and tile imitation ancient courtyard houses, this is also one of the larger surviving Qing Dynasty ancient streets in Chengdu. In the 57th year of Kangxi (AD 1718), after suppressing the Dzungar rebellion, over a thousand soldiers were stationed in Chengdu, and based on the former Xiao City foundation, the Manchu City was built. At that time, Wide Alley was named Xingren Hutong, Narrow Alley Tai Ping Hutong, and Well Alley Ruyi Hutong (also called De Ming Hutong).
"Shu Feng Ya Yun" Sichuan Opera Face-Changing
Watch a splendid Sichuan opera and experience authentic Sichuan folk art.
Shu Feng flows for thousands of years, Ya Yun passes down for a hundred years. Shu Feng Ya Yun is located in the ancient architectural complex of Qintai Gu Dao, Qingyang Palace, Chengdu, Sichuan. Adjacent to the renowned Qingyang Palace across the wall, and in distant response to Du Fu's Thatched Cottage and Wuhou Shrine. The architecture of Shu Feng Ya Yun is ancient and simple, embodying the sturdy, leisurely spirit of ancient Western Sichuan, once a flourishing and declining hundred-year-old pear garden (opera troupe). For a long time, it has been a gathering place for renowned Sichuan opera performers. It is a Sichuan folk pear garden with deep historical roots.

📅 Day 4: Chengdu a Dujiangyan a Qingcheng Mountain a Chengdu
Dujiangyan -> Qingcheng Mountain

📍 09:00-10:30 | Dujiangyan
Suggested Duration: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Dujiangyan is the world's oldest, only surviving, and grandest water conservancy project featuring damless water diversion. It mainly consists of three parts: the Fish Mouth Water Dividing Dyke, the Flying Sand Weir Flood Discharge Channel, and the Bottle-Neck Inlet. It has continuously functioned for flood control and irrigation. The surrounding scenery of Dujiangyan is beautiful, with numerous cultural relics and historic sites, mainly including Fulong Guan, Erwang Temple, Anlan Suspension Bridge, Yulei Pass, and Lidui Park.
Upon arrival at Dujiangyan, enter the scenic area from the mid-slope entrance of Yulei Mountain, take the Step Cloud Corridor escalator to the top of Yulei Pavilion (escalator fee of 40 yuan/person to be self-paid) to overlook the panoramic view of the Dujiangyan water conservancy project, learn about the water conservancy project, and gaze upon the flat Dujiangyan urban area located on the plain. Then proceed downstream, passing by the Erwang Temple commemorating Li Bing and his son, then the Anlan Suspension Bridge, and sequentially visit the Fish Mouth Water Dividing Dyke, the Flying Sand Weir Flood Discharge Channel (requires self-paid scenic area sightseeing car fee of 10 yuan/person), and the Bottle-Neck Water Inlet, restoring the full picture of the three major water conservancy projects from details. After exiting via Lidui Park, Zhangsong Ginkgo, Prostrate Iron, and Weir Function Road, leave the Dujiangyan scenic area, then take the Guanxian Ancient City sightseeing car (requires self-paid 10 yuan/person) to the parking lot gathering point.

📍 17:00-19:30 | Qingcheng Mountain
Suggested Duration: Approximately 2 hours 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Qingcheng Mountain is located in the southwest of Dujiangyan City, Chengdu, Sichuan Province, 68 kilometers east of Chengdu urban area, and 10 kilometers southwest of the Dujiangyan water conservancy project. The scenic area covers 200 square kilometers, with the peak of Laozi Pavilion at an altitude of 1260 meters. Qingcheng Mountain is divided into front and back mountains, with peaks surrounding and undulating, lush and verdant forests, enjoying
the reputation of 'Qingcheng, the most secluded under heaven'. The entire mountain is lush and green year-round, with peaks standing in opposition, resembling city walls, hence the name Qingcheng Mountain. Red ladders with thousands of steps, winding paths leading to seclusion, winning with tranquility and purity. Both inside and outside the scenic area, the Tianshi Cave and Yuanming Palace are secluded and tranquil, a major feature of Qingcheng Mountain.
Enter the scenic area through the mountain gate, breathe in the fresh air, climb step by step, ascend high and look afar, and before your eyes appears lush and verdant forests, layered peaks and overlapping barriers. After passing through the Rain Pavilion, Natural Pavilion, Joyful Nest, and Guiding Victory Pavilion, you arrive at the Natural Painting Workshop. Alternatively, take a boat across Yuecheng Lake and then a cable car to Shangqing Palace.
Note: Self-arranged local transportation as needed; does not include round-trip sightseeing car (20 yuan/person), front mountain cable car round-trip (60 yuan/person), or Yuecheng Lake boat ticket (5 yuan/person).
