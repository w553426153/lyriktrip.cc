📍 14:30-17:30 | Forbidden City Museum
Suggested Duration: About 3 hours
Attraction Overview
Explore one of the largest ancient royal palace architectural complexes in the world—the Forbidden City—including the main ticket (3-hour in-depth tour), and experience the grandeur and luxury of the imperial palace.
The Forbidden City is located in the center of Beijing, formerly known as the Purple Forbidden City. Completed in the 18th year of Yongle in the Ming Dynasty (1420), it served as the imperial palace for the Ming and Qing dynasties, an unparalleled masterpiece of ancient architecture, and the largest and most complete existing wooden structure ancient building complex in the world. All buildings in the Forbidden City consist of the 'Outer Court' and 'Inner Court,' surrounded by city walls. The moat, known as the Tongzi River, encircles all four sides. Corner towers stand at each of the four corners, with one gate on each side; the main gate is the Meridian Gate (Wumen) to the south.
