📍 10:30-11:50 | 【Temple of Heaven Park】(Combo Ticket: Includes Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, Echo Wall, Circular Mound Altar)
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 20 minutes
Attraction Overview
Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site 【Temple of Heaven Park】(Combo Ticket: Includes Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, Echo Wall, Circular Mound Altar) to pray for a beautiful tomorrow.
It was the sacred site where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties prayed for good harvests in early summer, rain in midsummer, and worshipped heaven at winter solstice. Located in the southeast part of the former outer city of Beijing, south of the Forbidden City and east of Zhengyangmen Outer, it was first built in the 18th year of the Yongle reign of the Ming Dynasty (1420) and served as the place where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties offered sacrifices to heaven.

Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Head to the city’s central square — 【Tiananmen Square】, located in the heart of Beijing, stretching 880 meters north-south and 500 meters east-west, covering 440,000 square meters, capable of accommodating up to 1 million people for large gatherings. Tiananmen Square was once the imperial court plaza outside the Meridian Gate of the Forbidden City during feudal rule, with the east, west, and south sides enclosed by walls, forming a restricted area for ordinary citizens.

📍 11:00-17:30 | Monument to the People's Heroes
Suggested Duration: About 10 minutes
Attraction Overview
【Monument to the People's Heroes】is located at the center of Tiananmen Square in Beijing. On September 30, 1949, the First Plenary Session of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference decided to erect the Monument to the People's Heroes in the capital, Beijing, to commemorate those who sacrificed their lives in the People's Liberation War and the People's Revolution. That afternoon at 6:00 PM, all attendees of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference held a groundbreaking ceremony for the monument on Tiananmen Square. After nationwide discussion, the monument design was finalized. In 1961, the Monument to the People's Heroes was listed by the State Council of the People's Republic of China as one of the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units. The Monument to the People's Heroes stands north of the Mao Zedong Memorial Hall and was erected by the People's Republic of China government to commemorate revolutionary martyrs in modern Chinese history.

📍 13:00-15:00 | Badaling Great Wall or Mutianyu Great Wall (Choose One)
Suggested Duration: About 2 hours
Attraction Overview
【Badaling Great Wall】is a particularly exquisite section of the Great Wall, combining majestic steepness with lush beauty, ranking first among China’s top ten scenic areas. In 1972, U.S. President Nixon said on the Great Wall: “Only a great nation could build such a great wall.” In 2009, U.S. President Obama similarly said on the Great Wall: “Here, we must express our deepest respect for Chinese civilization.”
【Mutianyu Great Wall】is located in Huairou District, Beijing, 73 kilometers from the city center. It boasts a long history and brilliant culture, enjoying the reputation both domestically and internationally of “The Ten-Thousand-Li Great Wall, Mutianyu Stands Alone.” The scenic area features overlapping mountains and vegetation coverage exceeding 90%. The Great Wall stretches 5,400 meters, is one of Beijing’s famous Sixteen Scenic Spots, and is a national AAAA-level tourist attraction.

📍 21:00-22:30 | Prince Gong's Mansion
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Prince Gong's Mansion is a Qing dynasty princely residence complex, which once served as the residence of Heshen, a powerful official during the Qianlong era, and Prince Qingxi Aisin Gioro Yonglin during the Jiaqing era. In the first year of the Xianfeng reign (1851), the Qing court bestowed this residence upon Prince Gong Aisin Gioro Yixin, after whom Prince Gong's Mansion is named.
Prince Gong's Mansion is grand in scale, covering a total area of 60,000 square meters, divided into two parts: the residence and the garden, with over 30 architectural complexes. It has witnessed the rise and fall of the Qing Dynasty, carrying rich historical and cultural information, hence the saying: 'One Prince Gong's Mansion, half of Qing Dynasty history.'

📍 23:00-23:30 | Shichahai
Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Shichahai is a historical and cultural tourism scenic area and a historical and cultural protection zone in Beijing. It includes the three water bodies of Qianhai, Houhai, and Xihai (also known as Jishuitan), along with surrounding areas, corresponding to the 'Front Three Seas,' commonly referred to as the 'Back Three Seas.' Many ancient buildings in the Shichahai scenic area hold significant importance in Beijing's urban development history and political and cultural history. Key representatives include Prince Gong's Mansion and Garden, ***Residence and Prince Chun's Mansion, Guo Moruo Memorial Hall, Bell and Drum
