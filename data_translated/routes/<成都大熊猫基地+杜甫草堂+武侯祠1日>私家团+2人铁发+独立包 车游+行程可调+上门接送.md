📍 13:00-14:30 | Du Fu Thatched Cottage including first admission ticket
Suggested Duration: about 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Du Fu Thatched Cottage is the former residence of the great Tang Dynasty poet Du Fu during his stay in Chengdu. At that time, Du Fu fled to Chengdu with his family to avoid the "An-Shi Rebellion",
and with the help of friends, built a thatched cottage to live in, known as the "Chengdu Thatched Cottage". Du Fu lived here for nearly four years, creating many widely cherished poems such as "Joyful Rain on a Spring Night" and "Song of the Thatched Hut Torn by Autumn Winds". The architecture here is simple and elegant, the garden is tranquil and beautiful, possessing both classical charm and a poetic atmosphere.
【Travel Tips (for reference only)】
1、Tour route: Main Gate → Da Wu → Poetry History Hall → Chai Men → Gong Bu Ci → Shaoling Thatched Cottage Stele Pavilion → Thatched Cottage Former Residence → Tang Dynasty Relics → Thousand Poems Stele → Bonsai Garden → Flower Path → Shadow Wall → Da Ya Hall → South Gate
2、 Highlights of the Cottage:
√ Thatched Cottage Former Residence: allows visitors to directly experience the scene of Du Fu's life at that time.
√ Shaoling Thatched Cottage Stele Pavilion: "Shaoling Thatched Cottage" is one of the iconic landscapes.
√ Poetry History Hall: displays Du Fu's poems and life experiences.
√ Gong Bu Ci: named after Du Fu's former position as an external official in the Ministry of Works, it has commemorative significance.
√ Tang Dynasty Relics: enables visitors to appreciate some remnants of Tang Dynasty scenery.
√ Da Ya Hall: features large colored-glaze murals and statues of many famous poets, very impressive.
√ Flower Path: red walls on both sides, full of artistic conception, ideal for photo-taking and check-in.
√ Bonsai Garden: diverse bonsai art forms, showcasing the beauty of gardens.
√ Shadow Wall: possesses unique cultural charm.

📍 17:00-18:30 | Jinli Ancient Street
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Jinli Ancient Street: Strolling through it, you can appreciate the ancient-style architecture and street atmosphere. Visit various specialty shops along the street, such as traditional handicraft stores and snack shops, where you can taste sugar-oil fruits, San Da Pao, and other delicacies. In the middle section of the ancient street, there are some ancient theater stages and other buildings suitable for photo-taking.
【Travel Tips (For Reference Only)】
Here are some food recommendations for Jinli Ancient Street:
1. San Da Pao: Made from glutinous rice, with red sugar steamed and melted, then mixed with finely ground fragrant powder of fried yellow beans and sesame, offering a sweet and delicious taste.
2. Beef Jiaobie: Made with beef butter scalded dough wrapping beef filling, then formed into a cake and pan-fried, resulting in a crispy, fragrant, and fresh skin.
3. Spicy Lianggao: Available throughout Jinli Ancient Street, it looks spicy but is actually not too spicy, with perfect color, aroma, and flavor.
4. Feichang Fen: Spicy, fresh, and refreshing, served with special mixed offal, suitable as a snack or a full meal.
5. Tian Shui Mian: Thick noodles paired with sweet and spicy sauce, offering a chewy texture and unique flavor.
6. Tang You Guo Zi: Made from glutinous rice, sesame, and sugar, with a crispy texture, sweet but not greasy.
7. Bo Bo Chicken: Various meat and vegetarian ingredients skewered on bamboo sticks and soaked in a spicy, fresh, and fragrant sauce, delivering a rich flavor.
8. Ye Er Ba: Made with glutinous rice flour wrapped around fillings such as minced pork and pickled vegetables, then steamed and eaten, offering a soft, sticky texture and delicious taste.
9. Dan Huang Gao: Made from eggs, flour, and sugar, baked until the outer skin is crispy and the filling is fragrant and sweet.
10. Boluo Fan: Pineapple flesh steamed together with glutinous rice, combining the sweet and sour taste of pineapple with the fresh aroma of glutinous rice.
