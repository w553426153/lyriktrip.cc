📍 09:00-11:00 | Free Activity Scenic Recommendation—Zhangjiajie Seventy-Two Odd Floors
Suggested Duration: About 2 hours
Attraction Overview
Evening self-guided visit to [Seventy-Two Odd Floors] in the Wuling Mountains. Since ancient times, there has been a legend of 'Nine Bows, Eighteen Villages, Seventy-Two Odd Floors.' The Seventy-Two Odd Floors are actually stilted buildings used by ethnic minority youths in western Hunan for meetings and defense. The 109.9-meter-tall cave stilted building, with its unique architectural form, distinctive hollow doorways, and multiple 'quirky' floor levels, has created a miracle in the history of stilted building architecture worldwide. The Odd Floors are distinctive in character, rich in business formats, offering abundant food and fun, with ever-changing light shows, dazzling and diverse. Step into this place, and every three steps reveals a small scene, every five steps a grand scene—steps shift the view, each step brings surprise. 'In short, here you will experience seventy-two different novel sensations, see seventy-two transformations—hence the name Seventy-Two Odd Floors.' From afar, the Seventy-Two Odd Floors look like a Tujia mountain village floating in mid-air—illusory yet real, as if in a dreamlike realm.

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is located in the northwestern part of Zhangjiajie City, Hunan Province. Yuanjiajie covers approximately 1,200 hectares with an average elevation of 1,074 meters, serving as a primary filming location for the movie Avatar. 📍 17:00-17:10 | No. 1 Bridge Under Heaven
Suggested Duration: 约10分钟
Attraction Overview
The 'No. 1 Bridge Under Heaven' is a natural stone bridge located in the Yuanjiajie Scenic Area of Zhangjiajie, situated in the Shadao Gou scenic zone, and is one of Zhangjiajie's premier attractions. Standing at 350 meters high, it is the tallest stone bridge in Zhangjiajie. Its height, span, and daring nature are exceptionally rare worldwide, hence its name 'No. 1 Bridge Under Heaven'.

📍 19:00-19:10 | Qiankun Pillar
Suggested Duration: 约10分钟
Attraction Overview
Qiankun Pillar is one of the "Three Thousand Odd Peaks" in Zhangjiajie, located at the southern end of the Yuanjiajie Scenic Area in the Wulingyuan Scenic and Historic Interest Area, with an elevation of 1,074 meters and a vertical height of approximately 150 meters. The summit is lush with vegetation, the peak has a unique shape, and the vertical joints are clearly cut, resembling being hewn by axe and chisel, standing tall and majestic in Zhangjiajie, possessing the aura of supporting heaven and earth, hence its name Qiankun Pillar. Qiankun Pillar also served as the real-world prototype for the background of the movie *Avatar*, and is therefore also known as the "Hallelujah Floating Mountain".

📍 11:30-11:40 | Yangjiajie Cable Car
Suggested Duration: Approximately 10 minutes
Attraction Overview
The Yangjiajie Cable Car is the third passenger cable car in the Wulingyuan World Natural Heritage Scenic Area, following the Tianzi Mountain and Huangshi Village cable cars. The upper station is located at Liujia Yan on the Bajiepo Tourist Route, and the lower station is at Zhonghu Xiang Xiekou Village. It uses French Poma cable car equipment, featuring a dual-loop circulating eight-passenger cabin configuration, with a total length of 1876 meters, a vertical elevation difference of 517 meters between upper and lower stations, 14 support towers, an operating speed of 6 m/s, a capacity of 2300 people/hour, and a one-way travel time of 5.2 minutes — currently an internationally advanced passenger cable car.
Note: Complimentary downhill ride on Yangjiajie Cable Car; no fee if not participating — refundable!

📍 15:00-15:30 | Tianmen Cave
Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
【Tianmen Cave】 (Included) Located on a cliff at over 1,260 meters above sea level, the cave is 131.5 meters high, 57 meters wide, and over 60 meters deep. According to geological experts, the central part of the cave is where east-west rock strata obliquely intersect; due to compression, the rock fractured and collapsed, forming the cave in 263 AD. As a world-high-altitude natural mountain-penetrating karst cave, Tianmen Cave hangs above a sheer cliff like a heavenly gate embedded in the sky.

📍 17:00-17:30 | Tianmen Mountain Lingquan Spring
Suggested Duration: About 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Lingquan Spring, located to the east of Tianmen Mountain’s summit, is said to emerge from rock fissures on the left side of the summit, flow into a pool, overflow into a sloping channel, and cascade down to the mouth of Tianmen Cave — this is the 'Plum Blossom Rain'. Lingquan Spring is cool and sweet, with a modest flow but never runs dry year-round. Locals believe its water possesses eight virtues: drinking it brings one clarity, two coolness, three fragrance, four softness, five sweetness, six purity, seven freedom from hunger, and eight freedom from thirst.

📍 20:50-21:50 | Furong Town
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour
Attraction Overview
Furong Town is Wang Village in Yongshun County, Xiangxi Tujia and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Hunan Province. It was once the capital of the Tujia kings during the Qin and Han dynasties, historically known as Youyang, and later as Xizhou during the Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period. Located on the north bank of the You River, it served as a key port for Youyang, connecting to Chuan-Qian and reaching E-Lu, offering excellent water transport advantages — a natural blessing. In the early 1980s, it gained fame due to the film *Furong Town*, and has since been officially renamed Furong Town.
