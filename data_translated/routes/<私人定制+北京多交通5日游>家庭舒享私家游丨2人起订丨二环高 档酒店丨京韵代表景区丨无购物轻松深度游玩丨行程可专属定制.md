Attraction Overview
Visit the world's largest ancient royal palace architectural complex — [Forbidden City 3H In-Depth Tour] (includes Treasure Gallery or Clock Gallery) — and experience the luxurious grandeur of the royal palace.
The Forbidden City is located in the center of Beijing, formerly known as the Purple Forbidden City. It was completed in the 18th year of the Yongle reign of the Ming Dynasty (1420) and served as the imperial palace for both the Ming and Qing dynasties. An unparalleled masterpiece of ancient architecture, it is the largest and most complete existing wooden-structured ancient building complex in the world. All buildings in the Forbidden City are divided into two parts: the 'Outer Court' and the 'Inner Court,' surrounded by city walls. The complex is encircled by the moat (Tongzi River) on all four sides. Corner towers stand at each of the four corners, and each side has one gate; the main southern gate is the Meridian Gate, which is the main entrance to the Forbidden City.

📍 17:00-18:30 | Prince Gong's Mansion
Suggested Duration: About 1 hour 30 minutes
Attraction Overview
Prince Gong's Mansion is located at Qianhai West Street, Xicheng District, Beijing. It is one of the best-preserved princely mansion complexes in China, divided into two parts: the residence and the garden, with the residence in front and the garden at the back. The mansion covers an area of approximately 31,000 square meters and is divided into three routes—central, eastern, and western—consisting of multiple courtyards arranged along a strict central axis.
The residence is not only spacious but also built to a high architectural standard, with clear indicators being the facade design and the number of rooms.
The Prince's Mansion has five bays on the front facade, seven bays in the main hall, five bays in the rear hall, seven bays in the rear chambers, and auxiliary halls on both sides. Mansions of princes and nobles below the rank of prince must not exceed these numbers. The architectural form and roof tile colors are also strictly regulated and cannot be altered. The Gong Wang Fu (Prince Gong's Mansion) has three courtyards each on the central, eastern, and western routes, with the last two courtyards on each route being the main areas for visitors to tour.
